Dynamic Policy

November 29, 2024

A policy that adapts its permissions based on specific conditions or contexts.

Description

In the context of Identity and Access Management (IAM), a dynamic policy refers to a set of rules that can change its behavior and permissions based on certain criteria or runtime context. Unlike static policies, which have fixed conditions and permissions, dynamic policies allow for more flexible and context-aware access control. This adaptability is crucial in environments where access needs can change rapidly depending on the scenario, such as in cloud computing or multi-tenant applications. Dynamic policies often leverage attributes such as user roles, location, time of access, or device type to make decisions. They are implemented using tools like policy engines that evaluate these attributes at the time of access request. This approach enhances security by ensuring that permissions are granted in a precise manner, reducing the risk of over-permission and unauthorized access.

Examples

  • A policy that grants access to a document only during business hours.
  • A policy that allows access to resources only if the request comes from a corporate network.

Additional Information

  • Dynamic policies are often used in Attribute-Based Access Control (ABAC) systems.
  • They can be integrated with real-time data sources to make instantaneous access decisions.
