Identity Resolution

November 29, 2024

The process of accurately identifying and consolidating a user's identity across multiple touchpoints and systems.

Description

Identity Resolution in the context of Identity and Access Management (IAM) refers to the process of linking and consolidating a user's identity data across various platforms, channels, or databases. This process ensures that different data points and identifiers corresponding to the same individual are accurately merged, providing a cohesive and singular view of the person’s identity. In modern digital ecosystems, users interact with systems through a multitude of devices and platforms, often resulting in fragmented identity data. By employing identity resolution, organizations can improve security, personalize user experiences, and ensure compliance with data protection regulations. Techniques used in identity resolution may include deterministic matching, where unique identifiers are used, and probabilistic matching, where algorithms deduce matches based on various data attributes.

Examples

  • A user logs into an e-commerce site using different email addresses; identity resolution merges these into a single profile.
  • An organization uses identity resolution to ensure that a customer's social media interactions are linked to their existing account in the CRM system.

Additional Information

  • Identity resolution helps in reducing redundant data, thereby optimizing storage and processing resources.
  • It is crucial for enabling seamless omnichannel user experiences, as it ensures consistent identity recognition across touchpoints.
