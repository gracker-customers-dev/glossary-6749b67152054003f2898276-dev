Credential Abuse

November 29, 2024

The misuse of legitimate user credentials to gain unauthorized access to systems or data.

Description

Credential abuse in the context of Identity and Access Management (IAM) refers to the exploitation of valid usernames and passwords by unauthorized individuals to access protected systems and information. This type of abuse typically occurs when attackers obtain credentials through methods such as phishing, social engineering, or data breaches. Once in possession of these credentials, attackers can impersonate legitimate users, bypass security measures, and gain access to sensitive data, leading to potential data breaches, financial loss, or reputational damage. Organizations often struggle with detecting credential abuse because the use of legitimate credentials may not trigger alerts in traditional security systems. To combat this threat, it's crucial for organizations to implement strong authentication measures, such as multi-factor authentication (MFA), and monitor user behavior for anomalies that may indicate compromised accounts.

Examples

  • An attacker uses a stolen username and password to log into a victim's email account and send phishing emails to their contacts.
  • A cybercriminal gains access to a company's internal network by using credentials obtained from a dark web marketplace, allowing them to exfiltrate sensitive data.

Additional Information

  • Credential abuse is a common technique in cyber attacks, often used in conjunction with other methods like social engineering.
  • Implementing security measures such as password policies, MFA, and user behavior analytics can help mitigate the risk of credential abuse.
