Biometric Authentication

November 29, 2024

A security process that relies on the unique biological characteristics of an individual to verify their identity.

Description

Biometric Authentication is a method used in Identity and Access Management (IAM) to verify an individual's identity based on their unique biological traits. Unlike traditional authentication methods such as passwords or PINs, biometric authentication uses physical characteristics like fingerprints, facial recognition, iris scans, or voice patterns. This approach enhances security by making it more difficult for unauthorized users to gain access, as these biological traits are unique to each individual and generally harder to replicate or steal. Biometric systems typically involve capturing and storing a biometric sample, such as a fingerprint scan, which is then used as a reference for future authentication attempts. The use of biometric authentication is expanding across various applications, including mobile devices, banking, and secure facility access, due to its convenience and increased security. However, there are concerns about privacy and the potential misuse of biometric data, which need to be addressed to ensure user trust and compliance with data protection regulations.

Examples

  • Using fingerprint recognition to unlock a smartphone.
  • Accessing a secure building using facial recognition technology.

Additional Information

  • Biometric data is often stored in a secure database and may be encrypted to protect user privacy.
  • Biometric authentication systems must be designed to minimize false positives and false negatives to ensure accurate identity verification.
