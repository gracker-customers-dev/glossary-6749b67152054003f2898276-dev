Identity Orchestration

November 29, 2024

Identity Orchestration is the process of managing and coordinating identity and access management workflows across multiple systems and applications.

Description

Identity Orchestration in the context of Identity and Access Management (IAM) refers to the automated coordination and management of identity-related workflows and processes across various systems, applications, and services. It involves integrating disparate identity systems to ensure seamless and secure access control, user provisioning, authentication, and authorization across an organization. By using orchestration, organizations can streamline identity management operations, reduce manual intervention, and improve security by ensuring consistent policy enforcement and compliance. It enables the dynamic adjustment of identity and access policies in response to changing business requirements or threat landscapes. Identity Orchestration often involves the use of orchestration platforms or tools that provide a centralized interface for configuring and managing identity workflows, supporting a wide range of protocols and standards to facilitate interoperability between different systems.

Examples

  • Automating user provisioning and deprovisioning across enterprise applications.
  • Coordinating multi-factor authentication processes for accessing cloud services.

Additional Information

  • Identity Orchestration enhances security by ensuring consistent policy enforcement.
  • It helps reduce operational costs by minimizing manual identity management tasks.
