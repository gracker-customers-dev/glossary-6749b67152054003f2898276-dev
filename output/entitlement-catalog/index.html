Entitlement Catalog

November 29, 2024

A structured collection of access rights and permissions available within an Identity and Access Management (IAM) system.

Description

An Entitlement Catalog in the context of Identity and Access Management (IAM) is a centralized repository that lists all the access rights, roles, and permissions that users can be granted within an organizationâ€™s IT environment. This catalog serves as a reference guide for managing user entitlements across various systems and applications. It helps standardize the process of assigning and revoking access, ensuring that users have appropriate permissions based on their roles. The catalog can include specific entitlements like access to applications, databases, and network resources, and is often used as part of an organization's governance and compliance framework. By maintaining a comprehensive entitlement catalog, organizations can improve security, reduce risk, and ensure regulatory compliance by providing visibility into who has access to what resources. Additionally, it facilitates the automation of access provisioning and deprovisioning processes, thereby enhancing operational efficiency.

Examples

  • A catalog listing access rights for a cloud service like AWS, detailing permissions for various roles such as admin, developer, and auditor.
  • An internal corporate catalog that includes permissions for accessing HR systems, finance applications, and customer databases.

Additional Information

  • The entitlement catalog is a key component in role-based access control (RBAC) systems, where roles are mapped to specific entitlements.
  • Regular audits of the entitlement catalog help ensure that access rights are up to date and align with current business needs and regulatory requirements.
