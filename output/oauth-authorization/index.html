OAuth Authorization

November 29, 2024

An open standard protocol for token-based authentication and authorization on the internet.

Description

OAuth Authorization is a widely adopted protocol that allows third-party services to exchange information without the need for users to share their credentials, typically usernames and passwords. It works by delegating access to a resource from one service to another on behalf of the user, through the issuance of access tokens. These tokens are used to authenticate and authorize requests, granting limited access to resources based on the permissions defined by the user. OAuth is commonly used to enable users to log in to a service using their credentials from another service, such as allowing users to sign in to an application using their Google or Facebook accounts. This process helps enhance security by minimizing password sharing and exposing only a limited scope of user data to third-party applications. OAuth is integral in today's web applications, mobile apps, and APIs for handling authorization in a secure and user-friendly manner.

Examples

  • Allowing a third-party app to access your Google Drive files without sharing your Google password.
  • Using your Facebook account to log into a new app, which then has access to your profile information.

Additional Information

  • OAuth 2.0 is the most current version of the protocol, providing better security features and simplified flows compared to OAuth 1.0.
  • OAuth is not an authentication protocol but is often used with OpenID Connect, which adds an identity layer for authentication.
