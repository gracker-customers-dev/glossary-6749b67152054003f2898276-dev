Identity Assurance Level

November 29, 2024

A measure of confidence in the validity of a user's claimed identity.

Description

Identity Assurance Level (IAL) refers to the degree of confidence that an organization has in the identity of a user. This concept is crucial in Identity and Access Management (IAM) as it helps organizations determine the necessary level of identity verification required for accessing specific resources or services. Identity assurance levels are typically defined through a combination of identity proofing processes, which can include verifying documents, biometric data, or other attributes that confirm an individual's identity. Different levels of assurance may be needed depending on the sensitivity of the resource being accessed. For example, a high assurance level might be required for accessing sensitive financial systems, whereas a lower assurance level might suffice for accessing an internal company portal. Establishing appropriate identity assurance levels helps mitigate the risk of unauthorized access and ensures that the right individuals are granted access to the right resources.

Examples

  • Example 1: A bank requires a high identity assurance level for online banking access, involving multi-factor authentication and document verification.
  • Example 2: An internal company portal requires a lower identity assurance level, where employees only need to log in with a username and password.

Additional Information

  • Additional info 1: Identity assurance levels can be influenced by regulations and compliance requirements specific to an industry.
  • Additional info 2: Organizations often categorize assurance levels into tiers, such as low, medium, and high, based on the risk associated with identity verification.
