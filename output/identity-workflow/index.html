Identity Workflow

November 29, 2024

A set of processes within an Identity and Access Management (IAM) system that automates and manages identity-related tasks.

Description

In the context of Identity and Access Management (IAM), an 'Identity Workflow' refers to a structured sequence of tasks and processes that an organization uses to manage the lifecycle of identities within its IT systems. This workflow encompasses various identity-related activities, such as user provisioning, de-provisioning, authentication, authorization, and role management. The goal of an identity workflow is to ensure that the right individuals have the appropriate access to resources at the right times for the right reasons. By automating these processes, organizations can enhance security, improve efficiency, and ensure compliance with regulatory requirements. Identity workflows can be tailored to suit the specific needs of an organization, integrating with other systems to provide a seamless user experience and reduce administrative overhead.

Examples

  • Automating user account creation when a new employee joins the organization.
  • Automatically revoking access when an employee leaves the company.

Additional Information

  • Identity workflows often integrate with HR systems to streamline the onboarding and offboarding process.
  • Advanced identity workflows can include multi-factor authentication steps to enhance security.
