Identity Lifecycle

November 29, 2024

The Identity Lifecycle refers to the management of a digital identity from creation to deactivation within an organization's IT environment.

Description

In the context of Identity and Access Management (IAM), the Identity Lifecycle encompasses all the stages that a digital identity undergoes during its existence within an organization. This lifecycle includes the creation of the identity when a new user or entity joins the organization, the maintenance and management of the identity as it is used to access various resources, and eventually the deactivation or deletion of the identity when it is no longer needed. Effective management of the Identity Lifecycle is crucial for ensuring that users have the appropriate access to resources at the right time while also maintaining security and compliance. This involves processes such as onboarding, role assignment, access provisioning, access review, and offboarding. Automating these processes can enhance efficiency and reduce errors. By closely monitoring the Identity Lifecycle, organizations can mitigate risks associated with unauthorized access and ensure that access rights are promptly updated as roles or employment statuses change.

Examples

  • An employee is onboarded, and their identity is created to give them access to necessary systems.
  • When an employee leaves the company, their identity is deactivated, and access to all resources is revoked.

Additional Information

  • Identity Lifecycle management can be automated using IAM tools to improve efficiency.
  • Regular audits of the Identity Lifecycle processes help ensure compliance with security policies.
