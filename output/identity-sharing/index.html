Identity Sharing

November 29, 2024

Identity Sharing refers to the practice of allowing multiple users or systems to access a shared identity for authentication and authorization purposes within an Identity and Access Management (IAM) framework.

Description

In the context of Identity and Access Management (IAM), Identity Sharing involves the use of a single identity by multiple users or systems to gain access to resources. This practice can simplify access management by reducing the number of individual identities that need to be maintained. However, it also introduces security and accountability challenges. When multiple users share the same identity, it becomes difficult to track actions back to a specific individual, thereby complicating auditing and compliance efforts. Identity Sharing is often used in scenarios where a shared resource needs to be accessed by a group, such as a generic account for a department or a service account used by multiple applications. To mitigate risks, organizations often implement controls such as logging, monitoring, and restricting the permissions associated with shared identities. Despite its convenience, Identity Sharing is generally discouraged in favor of individual identities that enhance security and traceability.

Examples

  • Using a generic email account for a support team that multiple employees access.
  • A shared service account used by several microservices to access a database.

Additional Information

  • Identity Sharing can lead to security risks due to the lack of individual accountability.
  • Best practices recommend using identity federation or role-based access control to avoid the need for Identity Sharing.
