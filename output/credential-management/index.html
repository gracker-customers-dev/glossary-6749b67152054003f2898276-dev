Credential Management

November 29, 2024

Credential Management refers to the processes and technologies used to manage, store, and safeguard the credentials required for authentication and access control within an identity and access management (IAM) system.

Description

Credential Management plays a crucial role in identity and access management (IAM) by ensuring that user credentials, such as passwords, tokens, certificates, and keys, are handled securely. Effective credential management involves the creation, distribution, storage, updating, and revocation of credentials. It aims to protect sensitive information from unauthorized access and reduce the risk of credential-related security breaches. Credential management can also involve the use of advanced technologies like single sign-on (SSO), passwordless authentication, and multi-factor authentication (MFA) to enhance security measures. Organizations implement credential management to comply with security policies and regulations, and to maintain the integrity and confidentiality of their systems. By automating and streamlining the credential management process, organizations can reduce the administrative overhead and minimize human error, while ensuring that credentials are protected throughout their lifecycle.

Examples

  • Implementing multi-factor authentication (MFA) to secure user accounts.
  • Using password managers to generate and store strong, unique passwords for users.

Additional Information

  • Credential management solutions often include features like password policy enforcement and credential rotation.
  • Integrating credential management with IAM systems can enhance security through centralized control and monitoring.
