Identity Trust

November 29, 2024

The level of confidence in the authenticity and integrity of an identity within an Identity and Access Management (IAM) system.

Description

Identity Trust in the context of Identity and Access Management (IAM) refers to the confidence in the legitimacy of a digital identity and its associated attributes. This concept is critical in IAM as it helps organizations determine how much access to grant a user based on the assurance level of their identity. Trust levels are often established through processes like identity verification, authentication, and continuous monitoring. Higher identity trust levels might be required for access to sensitive systems or data. Trust can be built through multi-factor authentication, identity proofing, and the establishment of a user's reputation over time in the system. Organizations often use identity trust to implement risk-based access controls, where the level of trust influences the permissions granted to a user. This approach helps to mitigate the risk of unauthorized access and data breaches by ensuring that only verified and trusted identities can access critical resources.

Examples

  • A bank uses high identity trust levels to allow users to access their financial accounts online after they have completed multi-factor authentication.
  • A corporate network grants access to sensitive data only to users who have verified their identity through a combination of identity proofing and device authentication.

Additional Information

  • Identity Trust is a key component in Zero Trust security models, where trust is never assumed, and verification is continuously required.
  • The level of identity trust required can vary depending on the sensitivity of the resource being accessed and the potential impact of unauthorized access.
