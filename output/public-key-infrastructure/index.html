Public Key Infrastructure

November 29, 2024

A framework for managing digital certificates and public-key encryption.

Description

Public Key Infrastructure (PKI) is a system of policies, technologies, standards, and procedures used to create, manage, distribute, store, and revoke digital certificates. It enables secure electronic transfer of information for a range of network activities such as e-commerce, internet banking, and confidential email. PKI is crucial for Identity and Access Management (IAM) as it provides a reliable way to authenticate the identity of users and devices. At the core of a PKI is the Certificate Authority (CA), which issues digital certificates that validate the ownership of a public key. PKIs also involve Registration Authorities (RAs), which verify the identity of entities requesting a certificate, and Certificate Revocation Lists (CRLs), which ensure that invalid or compromised certificates are not used. By facilitating secure communications and data integrity, PKI supports trust and authentication in digital environments.

Examples

  • An organization uses PKI to issue digital certificates to employees for secure email communication.
  • A website uses PKI to secure transactions by providing SSL/TLS certificates to encrypt data between the server and clients.

Additional Information

  • PKI supports encryption, digital signatures, and authentication.
  • A robust PKI implementation involves establishing a secure trust chain through hierarchical or mesh architectures.
