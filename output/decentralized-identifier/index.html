Decentralized Identifier

November 29, 2024

A globally unique identifier that is created and managed in a decentralized manner.

Description

Decentralized Identifiers (DIDs) are a new type of identifier that enables verifiable, self-sovereign digital identity. Unlike traditional identifiers such as email addresses or usernames, DIDs are not tied to a central authority, and they are not stored on a centralized server. Instead, they are registered on a distributed ledger or blockchain, which ensures their security and immutability. DIDs are designed to give individuals and organizations control over their own identity, allowing them to manage their personal data and credentials independently of any central authority. This self-sovereign approach enhances privacy and security by minimizing reliance on third parties. A DID can be associated with various cryptographic keys and service endpoints, enabling secure communication and authentication. The decentralized nature of DIDs supports interoperability across different systems and platforms, making it a foundational element for the development of the decentralized web and Internet of Things (IoT) applications.

Examples

  • A user creates a DID to manage their own identity independently of any social media platform.
  • A company uses DIDs to issue verifiable credentials to employees without relying on a centralized certificate authority.

Additional Information

  • DIDs are part of the W3C's efforts to standardize decentralized identity technologies.
  • DIDs can be resolved using a DID resolver, which retrieves the associated DID document from a blockchain or distributed ledger.
