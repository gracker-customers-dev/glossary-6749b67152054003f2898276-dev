Entitlement Review

November 29, 2024

A process in Identity and Access Management (IAM) to review and verify user access rights and permissions.

Description

Entitlement Review is an essential process within Identity and Access Management (IAM) that involves regularly reviewing users' access rights and permissions to ensure they are appropriate and compliant with security policies. This process is crucial for maintaining the principle of least privilege, where users are granted only the access necessary to perform their job functions. Entitlement Reviews help organizations identify and remediate unnecessary or excessive access, which can pose security risks. Typically conducted periodically, these reviews involve stakeholders such as managers, application owners, and auditors who assess whether the current access entitlements are justified and align with business requirements. By ensuring that access permissions are up-to-date and relevant, Entitlement Reviews play a key role in minimizing the risk of insider threats and unauthorized access, thereby enhancing the overall security posture of an organization.

Examples

  • Example 1: A quarterly review where managers assess and approve or revoke employee access to sensitive financial systems.
  • Example 2: An annual audit where IT security teams evaluate access permissions to critical infrastructure to ensure compliance with regulatory requirements.

Additional Information

  • Additional info 1: Entitlement Reviews can be automated using IAM tools that provide reporting and analytics to streamline the process.
  • Additional info 2: Regular Entitlement Reviews are often required for compliance with standards such as ISO 27001, GDPR, and SOX.
