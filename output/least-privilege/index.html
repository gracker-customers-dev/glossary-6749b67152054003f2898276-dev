Least Privilege

November 29, 2024

The principle of granting users only the permissions they need to perform their job functions.

Description

Least Privilege is a fundamental security principle in Identity and Access Management (IAM) that involves restricting access rights for users, accounts, and computing processes to only those resources absolutely necessary to perform their intended functions. The main goal of this principle is to minimize the potential damage from accidents, errors, or unauthorized use by limiting the access privileges granted. By implementing Least Privilege, organizations can reduce the attack surface, prevent lateral movement in case of breaches, and ensure that users have just enough permissions to accomplish their tasks, without overexposing sensitive systems or data. This approach enhances security by reducing potential exploitation vectors and ensuring more controlled access to critical resources.

Examples

  • Example 1: An employee in the finance department is given access only to the financial systems and data they need to perform their accounting tasks, rather than access to all company data.
  • Example 2: A database administrator is granted rights to manage database configurations and perform backups but not to access application code or user data directly.

Additional Information

  • Additional info 1: Implementing Least Privilege often involves regularly reviewing and adjusting user permissions to ensure they remain aligned with current job functions.
  • Additional info 2: Least Privilege is often complemented by role-based access control (RBAC), where users are assigned roles with predefined access rights to streamline permission management.
