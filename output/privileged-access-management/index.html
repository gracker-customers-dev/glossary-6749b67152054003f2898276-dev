Privileged Access Management

November 29, 2024

The process of managing and securing elevated access and permissions for users, accounts, and systems within an IT environment.

Description

Privileged Access Management (PAM) is a critical subset of Identity and Access Management (IAM) that focuses on the oversight and protection of accounts with elevated permissions. These accounts, often referred to as 'privileged accounts', have the ability to access sensitive data, change configurations, and manage resources, making them prime targets for cyberattacks. PAM solutions aim to mitigate risks by implementing stringent controls and monitoring mechanisms. Core components include credential vaulting, where passwords are securely stored and rotated; session monitoring, which tracks and records privileged account activity; and least privilege enforcement, ensuring users have only the access necessary for their roles. These controls help prevent unauthorized access and reduce the potential damage from compromised accounts. As organizations increasingly adopt cloud services and complex IT infrastructures, the role of PAM becomes more vital to safeguard against internal and external threats.

Examples

  • Example 1: A company uses a PAM solution to manage and rotate the passwords of their administrator accounts automatically, ensuring they remain secure.
  • Example 2: An organization implements session recording for all privileged access sessions, enabling them to audit activities and identify suspicious behavior.

Additional Information

  • Additional info 1: PAM is often integrated with other security solutions such as SIEM (Security Information and Event Management) to provide comprehensive security monitoring.
  • Additional info 2: Implementing PAM can help organizations comply with regulatory requirements by ensuring secure management of privileged accounts.
