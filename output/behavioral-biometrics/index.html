Behavioral Biometrics

November 29, 2024

Behavioral biometrics refers to the identification and authentication based on patterns in human activities.

Description

Behavioral biometrics is a security technology used in identity and access management (IAM) systems to identify and authenticate individuals based on unique patterns in their behavior. Unlike traditional biometrics that rely on physical attributes like fingerprints or facial recognition, behavioral biometrics focuses on how a person interacts with devices and systems. This can include keystroke dynamics, mouse movement patterns, touchscreen interactions, and even how someone walks or speaks. These behavioral patterns are difficult to replicate, making them a robust layer of security. Behavioral biometrics are particularly useful in continuously verifying a user's identity in real-time, reducing the risk of fraud and unauthorized access. They are often used in combination with other authentication factors to enhance security. For instance, in finance, they help detect unusual activity, potentially indicating fraud, by analyzing how a user typically performs transactions.

Examples

  • Keystroke dynamics: Identifying a user by their typing rhythm and speed.
  • Mouse movement analysis: Recognizing a user based on how they move the mouse.

Additional Information

  • Behavioral biometrics can work unobtrusively in the background, offering seamless security.
  • They are increasingly used in financial services to detect and prevent fraudulent activities.
