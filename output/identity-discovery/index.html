Identity Discovery

November 29, 2024

The process of identifying and cataloging all identities within an organization's IT environment.

Description

Identity Discovery is a critical aspect of Identity and Access Management (IAM) that involves systematically identifying and cataloging all user identities within an organization's IT environment. This process is essential for understanding who has access to what resources and ensuring that the right individuals have appropriate access based on their roles and responsibilities. Identity Discovery helps organizations detect and manage unmanaged or orphaned accounts, which can be a significant security risk. It involves scanning systems and applications to uncover all user accounts, including those that may have been overlooked or improperly decommissioned. By maintaining an up-to-date inventory of identities, organizations can enhance their security posture, comply with regulatory requirements, and improve operational efficiency. This process often involves the use of automated tools to continuously monitor and update identity information, ensuring that access controls are aligned with organizational policies and that any anomalies or unauthorized access can be quickly identified and addressed.

Examples

  • Identifying all user accounts in a cloud infrastructure to assess access control.
  • Running a discovery tool to find orphaned accounts in legacy systems.

Additional Information

  • Identity Discovery is often the first step in an IAM implementation project.
  • Automated identity discovery tools can integrate with existing IT systems for continuous monitoring.
