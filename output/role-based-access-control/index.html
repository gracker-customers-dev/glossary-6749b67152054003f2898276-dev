Role-Based Access Control

November 29, 2024

A method of restricting access to resources based on the roles of individual users within an organization.

Description

Role-Based Access Control (RBAC) is a policy-neutral access control mechanism defined around roles and privileges. The components of RBAC such as role-permissions, user-role and role-role relationships make it simple to perform user assignments. In RBAC, access decisions are based on the roles that individual users have as part of an organization. Users are assigned to roles based on their responsibilities and qualifications, and access to resources is limited to only those users who are authorized. This helps ensure that users only have the access necessary to perform their job functions, which minimizes security risks and potential for unauthorized access. RBAC is widely used in enterprise systems to manage access control in a systematic, repeatable, and scalable fashion.

Examples

  • An employee in the finance department is granted access to financial applications and data, but not to engineering resources.
  • A system administrator has broad access to system management tools but does not have access to sensitive user data.

Additional Information

  • RBAC can be implemented in various systems such as databases, operating systems, and applications.
  • RBAC supports the principle of least privilege by allowing users to access only the resources necessary for their roles.
