Decentralized Identity

November 29, 2024

A system of identity management that allows individuals to own and control their digital identities without relying on a central authority.

Description

Decentralized Identity is an approach to identity management that empowers users to create and manage their digital identities independently. This model leverages blockchain technology and distributed ledgers to enable individuals to possess and control their identity information, which is stored securely in digital wallets. Unlike traditional identity systems that rely on centralized authorities (such as government agencies or large corporations) to verify and store identity data, decentralized identity frameworks distribute this power to the users themselves. As a result, users can selectively share identity attributes with service providers, enhancing privacy and security. This model fosters interoperability across different platforms and services, reduces the risk of identity theft or data breaches, and aligns with principles of user autonomy and consent. Decentralized Identity is gaining traction as it addresses growing concerns about privacy and data security in the digital age.

Examples

  • A user creates a decentralized identity using a blockchain-based identity platform and uses it to log into a website without a traditional username and password.
  • A company allows its customers to verify their identity for services without storing personal information, relying instead on decentralized identifiers.

Additional Information

  • Decentralized Identity often involves the use of Decentralized Identifiers (DIDs) and Verifiable Credentials (VCs).
  • This approach can enhance user privacy by minimizing the amount of personal data shared and stored across different services.
